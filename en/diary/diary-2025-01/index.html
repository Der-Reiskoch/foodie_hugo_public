Khao Moo Grob (01-08)

For lunch today, I simply served the remainder of the second batch of crispy pork belly from the air fryer as “Khao Moo Grob” (ข้าวหมูกรอบ) along with jasmine rice and dips.

Pandan Experiments (01-05)

Tonight I wanted to see if I could make Thai pandan chicken, or “Kai Hor Bai Toey” (ไก่ใบเตย), in the air fryer. It worked very well, but unfortunately, most of my pandan leaves had gone moldy, so I could only make two small batches. With the remaining marinated chicken, which I had also prepared according to a recipe from Leela Punyaratabandhu’s book “Bangkok”, I spontaneously made a chicken salad, or “Yam Kai” (ยำไก่) in Thai.

Since I also had an open can of pandan extract, I used it to make mango sticky rice, or “Khao Niau Ma Muang” (ข้าวเหนียวมะม่วง), for dessert. Here i folloed a recipe from the “Hungry in Thailand” blog. Because I didn’t use any green food coloring, the sticky rice only turned pale green, but it had a very intense pandan flavor.

Recommended Bangkok at amazon.de*

Kai Thod Kratiam Grob (01-05)

I did some more air fryer experiments for lunch today. I’d marinated chicken drumsticks the previous evening, using a recipe from Leela Punyaratabandhu’s Cookbook “Bangkok”, and then cooked them up with fried garlic as “Kai Thod Kratiam Grob” (ไก่ทอดกระเทียมกรอบ). The drumsticks turned out great in the air fryer, but “frying” the garlic wasn’t quite as successful.

Recommended Bangkok at amazon.de*

Chicken wings and salad (01-04)

We had Thai chicken wings, or “Pik Kai Thod” (ปีกไก่ทอด), again tonight. This time using a recipe from Ben Kindler’s “Bangkok Original Streetfood” book, though cooked in the air fryer. We served it with a Thai salad - a “thief salad”, as I’d translate “Yam Khamoy” (ยำขโมย).

Recommended Bangkok Original Streetfood at amazon.de*

Khao Soi Gai (01-03)

Today I had Thai Khao Soi Gai (ข้าวซอยไก่) for both lunch and dinner. I had this Northern Thai noodle soup twice because I was experimenting with making the crispy noodles in the air fryer. I think I’ve now found a good method: spray a small portion of cooked noodles with oil and then leave them in the air fryer at 160° Celsius for about eight to ten minutes. Thanks to @thaifood_by_passi for the tip! By the way, the chicken drumsticks in the soup were also from the air fryer.

Recommended Air Fryer von Bella at amazon.de*

Fried Rice & Chicken Wings (01-02)

Tonight, I made Thai “Khao Pad Sapparot” (ข้าวผัดสับปะรด) using the last pineapple from our Christmas meal, leftover rice, and some shrimp. I also made a side of fried chicken wings, or “Pik Gai Thod” (ปีกไก่ทอด), which was another successful experiment in the air fryer. I loosely followed a recipe from Pailin Chongchitnant’s Hot Thai Kitchen cookbook for the pineapple fried rice.

Recommended Hot Thai Kitchen at amazon.de*

Rad Na Mee Grob (01-02)

Even though I was warned in advance that it likely wouldn’t be a success, I still tried another air fryer experiment for lunch today: Thai Rad Na Mee Grob (ราดหน้าหมี่กรอบ) with air-fried noodles. Sadly, the noodles were just as disappointing as when I try to deep-fry them.
